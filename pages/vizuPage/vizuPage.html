The Kinneret, Sea of Galilee, is in the northeast of Israel

The largest freshwater lake in Israel and the lowest in the world. In the past, Kinneret supplied about a quarter of Israel's water consumption, but due to the decline in water levels, it now supplies between 2 and 13 percent of total consumption.


This visualization shows the amount of precipitation and the water level in each month since 1978. Also, by clicking on the switch, you can switch to an annual display of the maximum level in a selected year and the total amount of precipitation in that year.
